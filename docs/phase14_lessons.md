## Working Notes

- Dual-anchor route/content contracts can preserve the stabilized late-route regime very well without reopening shortcut collapse, but that is not enough by itself to move held-confirm content.
- The first fully paneled dual-anchor family (`17031`) is useful because it removes one ambiguity cleanly: branch stability and held-confirm content lift are now separable again in phase 14, just as shortcut stability and held-confirm lift were separable in phase 13.
- The first fully paneled teacher-first content-only family (`17024`) now says the same thing from the mainline lane: content-branch-only supervision preserves the late-route regime and wins the content-failure hard slice, but still does not convert those summary-time gains into held-confirm recovery.
- The stronger teacher-first follow-up (`17025`) closes the same way, so simple teacher-only scaling is no longer a live frontier by itself.
- The remaining honest question is narrower than the broad cluster list suggests: whether hard-slice mining can turn its summary-time late-route content gains into confirm-time gains, or whether the phase-14 ceiling is already telling us the problem is deeper than branch-only supervision.
